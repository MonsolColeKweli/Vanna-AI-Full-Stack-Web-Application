Project Contributions

I focused mainly on front-end development, building out the core user interface and making sure the design was clean, modern, and simple to navigate. I also contributed to the back-end by helping connect the front end to APIs and handling basic server-side tasks. While I’m not a designer, I cared a lot about making the application feel good to use without taking away from its core functionality. I wanted the experience to be smooth and intuitive while still keeping everything efficient and practical. I also took an active role in project management by helping plan tasks, staying organized, and keeping communication clear.

Reflections & Insights

Working on this project was both a challenge and a great learning experience. One of the biggest things I took away was getting a better understanding of how the back end works, especially while trying to learn the Vanna API and wrap my head around Docker. It was definitely tough at times to visualize how all the different parts of the application connected and worked together behind the scenes. On top of that, figuring out a solid design for the pages took a lot of trial and error, and we ran into more than a few bugs along the way—some more frustrating than others. But in the end, the project really felt like a culmination of everything we’ve learned in class. As difficult and mind-boggling as it could be at times, I genuinely had fun working through the problems and seeing everything come together.
